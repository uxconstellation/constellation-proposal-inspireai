01 — The Opportunity

Market leader.
Generic website.
That's the gap.

InspireAI has the credentials — the Salesforce investment, the European deployment record, the 30-person team that punches well above its weight. The website doesn't reflect any of it. Visitors see a functional but forgettable B2B site with no sense of market leadership, no interactive proof of capability, and no reason to believe InspireAI is anything other than another AI consultancy.

The redesign doesn't just fix the aesthetics. It repositions InspireAI in the "edgy B2B" space — punchy, credible, and human — using interactive storytelling to show prospects how you actually build agents, not just tell them you do.

No differentiation

The current site reads like every other AI consultancy. There is nothing that communicates the actual scale of your deployment record or the challenger mentality that defines how InspireAI operates.

Wasted credibility signals

Salesforce and Slack partner badges exist but land awkwardly. The Salesforce investment is not surfaced at all. These are your strongest trust signals — they need to earn their position on the page.

No interactive proof

Telling prospects you build AI agents is easy. Showing them — through an interactive experience that walks them through your methodology — is what converts curiosity into a strategy call booking.

Copy doesn't reflect the brand

InspireAI's actual voice — confident, direct, results-focused — doesn't come through. The copy is safe where it should be pointed, and generic where it should be specific. The site needs to sound like you.
